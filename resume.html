Thesis Research: AI-Driven Lab-on-a-Chip

My thesis research represents a deep dive into the intersection of biomedical engineering, microfluidics, and artificial intelligence. The goal was to significantly enhance the accuracy of cytotoxicity detection for cancer immunotherapy research. The flow diagram below illustrates the comprehensive, multi-stage process I led, from initial design and fabrication to advanced AI-driven data analysis.

Project Workflow: Advancing Cytotoxicity Detection

1

Design & Fabrication

Designed and fabricated 5+ custom 3D-printed microfluidic chips and 2 novel lab-on-chip platforms in Fusion 360 to assess real-time cytotoxic responses of NK immune cells.

2

Experimentation

Conducted droplet-based microfluidic experiments and NK cell culture assays across 10+ in vitro tumor models, generating over 5,000 images for analysis.

3

AI Model Adaptation

Adapted and tuned a Detectron2 AI model for automated cytotoxicity event detection, manually labeling 200+ immune-tumor interaction frames to create a high-quality training dataset.

4

Data Analysis & Optimization

Built clean datasets and improved data quality from 5,000+ images by optimizing preprocessing and augmentation workflows, leading to robust model performance.
